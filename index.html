We study

Microbiome, immune homeostasis, and tissue-resident cell biology

Our lab studies how gut and skin commensal microbiota shape host immune homeostasis and disease. We develop gnotobiotic model systems and dysbiosis-driven inflammatory disease models to define the cells and mechanisms that connect microbes, tissues, and immunity.

Research Highlights

Three or four clear themes are enough to communicate the lab's research direction.

Gut microbiome and host homeostasis

We study how commensal microbes support balanced immune function and healthy tissue physiology in the host.

Keywords: gut microbiome, immune homeostasis, host physiology

Skin microbiota and allergic inflammation

We investigate how early-life microbial exposure and skin dysbiosis shape inflammatory pathways and allergic disease.

Keywords: skin microbiota, dysbiosis, allergic inflammation

Tissue-resident cells

We aim to identify tissue-resident cell populations whose development and differentiation are regulated by commensal microbiota.

Keywords: tissue-resident cells, differentiation, microbiota

Platform / Methods

Our work combines germ-free and gnotobiotic animal models, defined microbial colonization, dysbiosis models, and microbiome profiling.

gnotobiotic mice, microbiome analysis, inflammatory disease models
