Microbiome, immune homeostasis, and tissue-resident cell biology

Ryu Lab studies how gut and skin commensal microbiota shape host immune homeostasis and disease. We combine gnotobiotic model systems, dysbiosis-driven inflammatory disease models, and microbiome-based analysis to uncover the mechanisms linking tissues, microbes, and immunity.

Research

Research areas

Our work connects commensal microbiota, tissue-resident cells, and inflammatory disease across gut and skin systems.

Gut microbiome and host homeostasis

We study how commensal microbes support balanced immune function and healthy tissue physiology in the host.

gut microbiome homeostasis host physiology

Skin microbiota and allergic inflammation

We investigate how early-life microbial exposure and skin dysbiosis shape inflammatory pathways and allergic disease.

skin microbiota allergy dysbiosis

Tissue-resident cell biology

We aim to identify tissue-resident cell populations whose development and differentiation are regulated by commensal microbiota.

resident cells differentiation immunity
